Plate

The Project

Context

Design a responsive recipe web app that creates ease of use for the users and solves the problems they are facing with existing recipe apps

Problem

  • With other recipe websites, information can be overwhelming, not easily digestible and not cater to user’s unique scenario.
  • Information can be very generic and not tailored to specific needs.
  • You want to make nutritious meals without spending too much money.

About

Plate is a recipe responsive app that provides users with a streamlined and enjoyable experience, filtering options tailed to support the users desires, step by step instructions and video demonstrations, smart shopping list feature and .

Design process- Lean UX

User Research

User Interview

I interviewed real people who could be potential users of my product. This was done to provide quality insight into users fundamental needs, uncover their frustrations and everyday patterns when interacting with the product.

Quesions

  • How do you find recipes online?
  • How do you determine what you’ll cook for dinner during the week?
  • What problems or frustrations do you find when you go onto recipe sites?
  • Are there any dietary requirements you have, and if so, how easy is it to find recipes on these sites?
  • When you visit recipe sites, what prompts you to chose specific meals to make?
  • How do save recipes you’ll like to make from recipe apps?
  • What features would you like to see on a recipe app that will draw you to using it?

Patterns

I found that:

  • Users tend to find recipes ideas from social media such as, Instagram.
  • They dislike the ads that pop up on recipe websites.
  • They don’t want to spend too long in the kitchen making meals.
  • Reviews and images used is important on if the user will prepare a recipe.

User Persona

3 user personas were created from the findings of the user interviews.

Key Features

Plate is user centred in which produces a seamless navigation experience. It allows users to favourite, share, tailor, plan recipes for a personalised experience.

User Responsive Web App

As Plate is not only a native mobile app but a responsive web app, I have created designs that would adapt to different breakpoints.

Final Screens

Flow #1: Continue as a guest

Users are given 3 options before they use the app. They can sign up as a new member, sign in if they have not registered already or continue as a guest where they can browse the app without sharing their details. They are also given the opportunity to tailor the recipes presented to them by answering some questions on their preferences.

The homepage allows users to access an extensive range of recipes and tutorials and also to navigate all the services.

Homepage

Flow #2: Save a recipe

Before users are able to save a recipe, they must sign in. If they started off browsing the app as a guest, a popup will appear for them to either log in or sign up. Whist browsing a recipe, users adjust the serving size which will incrementally adjust the serving of each ingredient either up or down.

Recipie Page

Flow #3: Search and share a recipe

User can search for recipes and share it to friends and family. There are various ways in which users can share a recipes such as, Pinterest, Twitter, email and Facebook.

Flow #4: Leftover Options

Recipe Page

To reduce waste, users are given ideas on what they can make with the leftovers.

Flow #5: Extensive filtering option

Users can filter to narrow down your recipe choice. As they narrow down, the recipe choices becomes more specific to their needs.

Retrospect

My interpretation of the brief was to create an recipe website that is more modern, up to date, but also simple and clean for your everyday user. As things are becoming more digital and people are on their phones more, I wanted the app to be easy on the eye and clean- a little break away.

I believe I've met the project objectives as I designed my responsive web app around the needs, wants and frustrations or people who use recipe websites regularly or who are potential users. From my interviews, I was able to get a great understanding of I can work on.

Some of the roadblocks I encounter included the style/design of the app. I knew I wanted a clean look which can cater to a younger and older audience, but I felt conflicted with different design ideas that stemmed away from my initial idea. I wanted to create as much white space as possible with out having the images and text look squashed or lost within the screens.

What I would do differently nest time is play around and incorporate some fun illustrations and animations that will more engaging but not distracting for a user. I would also incorporate more user testing throughout the design process just so I have a better feel for what the users find eye catching with every screen.
