UNDERONE

THE BRIEF

Project Overview

About This Project.

This is a design process case study for UnderOne - a finance app that users to save and track their finances better-making banking easier.

My aim with this case study is to demonstrate my approach on how to design, and in particular how to wireframe, design and prototype an app for Android and iOS, adhering to its independent guidelines.

The Goal

The app is for users who want help with tracking their money, analysing their spending and want an easier access and visibility to all their bank accounts and financial data in a single view without jumping between apps. It simplifies the process of tracking and managing income and expenses by providing a comprehensive view.

Key Features

    - Log in with ease.

    - Add their different bank accounts to a single wallet.

    -Securely move money between bank accounts, and to friends and family.

    - Track their balances and transactions across their accounts, giving a clear picture of their financial situation.

MY APPROACH

User Flow

A consolidated user flow was developed to tie three key funcions together:

  • Login or sign up
  • Add a bank card to the wallet
  • Send money
  • View spending history
IDEATE

Wireframes

I referred to Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines and Google’s Material Design to design the wireframes.

To create the design differences between iOS and Android, I used user generated i0S UI Kit and Material Design Figma UI kit. These allowed me to create detailed wireframes that highlights the nuances between Android and i0S.

Takeaways

#1 Negative space

A challenge I faced when designing UnderOne was making sure there was enough white space. There were different features and elements I wanted to place on the homepage thinking it would be helpful for the user, but this would have raised the level of distraction and overload.

#2 Using Illustrations as the core visual element of UI design.

Illustrations can act as a powerful tool igniting users emotions from the first time they interact with them. They create the needed atmosphere of joy, pleasure and fun before the user starts reading. I like the design for this project, creating a fun, simple to use FinTech app.
